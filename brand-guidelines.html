Brand Guidelines

The single reference for how the club looks, sounds, and shows up. Logos, color, type, voice, and the rules that keep every touchpoint feeling like one studio.

01 — The essence

A new lens on the professional portrait.

The Headshot Club is a photographer collective in Boca Raton producing editorial grade headshots for the faces that earn their living on screen: actors, realtors, lawyers, founders, and executives across South Florida. The work is senior led, unhurried, and built for where the portrait actually lives.

What the club is

A collective, not a single chair. One standard of quality whoever is behind the lens. Editorial in craft, calm in delivery, professional in tone. By appointment only.

What the club is not

Not a volume mill, not a quick snap, not loud or salesy. The club never centers any one photographer by name in public facing material; the actor is always the club or the collective.

Usage rules

✓ Do

  • Use the light logo on Ink, charcoal, or photography. Use the dark logo on Bone and light backgrounds.
  • Keep clear space around the lockup equal to the height of the hc. mark on every side.
  • Hold the lockup to a minimum width of roughly 120px on screen so the wordmark stays legible.
  • Use the compact mark when the full lockup would be too small or too tight.
  • Place the logo on areas of calm, even contrast.

✕ Don't

  • Recolor the logo or swap the terracotta accent for another color.
  • Stretch, condense, rotate, or distort the proportions.
  • Add drop shadows, glows, outlines, or other effects.
  • Rebuild or rearrange the lockup, or set the name in a different typeface.
  • Place the logo on a busy photo or a low contrast color where it disappears.

05 — Voice & tone

Editorial, understated, confident.

The club sounds like a calm expert who has nothing to prove. Warm and professional, never loud or salesy. Short sentences. Plain words. No hyphens where a space will do.

✓ The voice is

  • Editorial and quietly confident
  • Warm, polite, and professional
  • Concise. One idea per line
  • Grounded in craft, not hype

✕ The voice is not

  • Salesy, urgent, or shouty
  • Jargon heavy or technical
  • Centered on any one photographer by name
  • Padded with filler or exclamation marks
Say this, not that
✓ "A new lens on the professional portrait."✕ "The BEST headshots in South Florida!!!"
✓ "Editorial grade headshots, by appointment."✕ "Book now and save big on headshot deals."
✓ "The club" or "the collective" as the actor.✕ Naming the individual photographer in public copy.
✓ "Delivered in a timely manner."✕ "Super fast turn-around!!"

06 — Photography & the signature element

Editorial light. A viewfinder frame.

Imagery is editorial grade: flattering natural light, real expressions, clean studio grounds from the background palette. Portraits look like the person on their best ordinary day, never a stranger holding still.

The signature mark

Thin corner brackets, like a camera viewfinder, frame layouts across social and print. Drawn from the logo, they turn a plain text slide into something unmistakably the club. Keep them thin, in terracotta or bone, with generous margins inside.
